## Developing Executive Presence

### The 3 Pillars of Executive Presence

**1. Communication Strength:**
- Clear, precise expression
- Convincing presentations
- Active listening at all levels

**2. Emotional Intelligence:**
- Recognize and manage your own emotions
- Understand others' perspectives and feelings
- Social competence

**3. Strategic Thinking:**
- Long-term perspective
- Systemic understanding
- Decision-making ability

### Body Language and Demeanor

**Non-verbal Signals:**
- **Open Posture:** Shoulders back, open stance
- **Eye Contact:** Confident but not staring
- **Gestures:** Natural and supportive
- **Voice:** Clear, modulated, self-assured

**Build Presence:**
- Enter room consciously
- Show presence before content
- Radiate authenticity

## Stakeholder Communication

### Stakeholder Mapping and Strategy

**Identify Stakeholders:**
- **Internal:** Employees, colleagues, superiors
- **External:** Customers, partners, investors, regulators
- **Influence vs. Interest:** Set priorities

**Communication Strategies:**
- **High Influence/High Interest:** Regular, detailed updates
- **High Influence/Low Interest:** Efficient, focused communication
- **Low Influence/High Interest:** Keep adequately informed
- **Low Influence/Low Interest:** Communicate minimally

### Effective Stakeholder Conversations

**Preparation:**
- Define conversation goals
- Understand stakeholder interests
- Prepare relevant data and facts
- Anticipate possible questions

**Execution:**
- Communicate clear agenda
- Listen actively and ask questions
- Share transparent information
- Make joint decisions

## Board Communication

### The Art of Board Presentations

**Board Expectations:**
- Strategic overview instead of operational details
- Clearly present risks and opportunities
- Emphasize long-term implications
- Provide recommendations

**Board Presentation Structure:**
1. **Executive Summary:** 2-minute overview
2. **Situation:** Current state and trends
3. **Strategy:** Planned measures
4. **Risks:** Challenges and mitigation
5. **Results:** Expected outcomes
6. **Recommendations:** Concrete action proposals

### Handling Board Questions

**Question Types:**
- **Factual Questions:** Have data and facts ready
- **Strategic Questions:** Show long-term perspective
- **Hypothetical Questions:** Demonstrate scenario planning
- **Difficult Questions:** Answer honestly, don't evade

**Techniques:**
- "That's a good question..."
- Separate facts from opinions
- "I'll look into that and get back to you"
- Communicate transparent uncertainties

## Handling Difficult Conversations

### The Structure of Difficult Conversations

**Preparation:**
- **Define Goals:** What do you want to achieve?
- **Change Perspective:** How does it look from the other side?
- **Gather Data:** Facts vs. interpretations
- **Plan Worst Case:** What's the worst scenario?

**Execution (CARE Model):**
- **Connect:** Build relationship
- **Analyze:** Understand problem together
- **Resolve:** Develop solutions
- **Execute:** Agree on next steps

### Specific Situations

**Giving Performance Feedback:**
- Name specific examples
- Separate behavior from person
- Emphasize development potential
- Offer support

**Resolving Conflicts:**
- Listen to both sides
- Find common interests
- Seek win-win solutions
- Agree on follow-up

**Termination Conversations:**
- Be empathetic and respectful
- Communicate clear reasons
- Offer support
- Maintain confidentiality

## Crisis Communication

### The 3 Phases of Crisis Communication

**1. Preparation:**
- Develop crisis communication plan
- Define spokespeople and channels
- Prepare key messages
- Update stakeholder lists

**2. During Crisis:**
- Communicate quickly and transparently
- Separate facts from speculation
- Show empathy
- Provide regular updates

**3. After Crisis:**
- Document lessons learned
- Evaluate communication
- Rebuild trust
- Improve processes

### Crisis Communication Principles

**Speed:**
- Communicate sooner rather than perfect
- Regular updates instead of waiting for perfection

**Transparency:**
- Communicate known information, investigate unknown as "under investigation"
- Don't speculate or deny rumors

**Empathy:**
- Take those affected seriously
- Emphasize human side
- Offer support

## Impactful Presentations

### The 4 Pillars of Strong Presentations

**1. Clear Structure:**
- Introduction with hook
- Main part with 3-5 key points
- Conclusion with call-to-action

**2. Strong Stories:**
- Connect data with stories
- Build emotional connection
- Create memorable messages

**3. Visual Support:**
- Less text, more images
- Clear diagrams and charts
- Professional design

**4. Convincing Delivery:**
- Natural presence
- Varied voice modulation
- Eye contact with audience

### Mastering Virtual Presentations

**Technical Preparation:**
- Test technology beforehand
- Ensure good lighting and background
- Use quality audio equipment

**Engagement Strategies:**
- Ask questions to keep others engaged
- Make eye contact with camera
- Use gestures and facial expressions

**Follow-up:**
- Provide recording for availability
- Offer Q&A separately
- Communicate action items
