## The 48-hour rule

<div class="persona-scenario">
<p>You're back from a conference. 40 new LinkedIn connections, 23 business cards, 8 "we should definitely talk again" promises. You know the first 48 hours decide whether anything comes of these encounters.</p>
<p>Monday passes with emails. Tuesday with meetings. By Wednesday, 35 of those contacts are already forgotten – in your inbox, on LinkedIn, on your desk. Of the remaining 12, you might call 2.</p>
<p><strong>The network you built decays without a system.</strong> And networking without follow-up is just collecting business cards.</p>
</div>

## After your next conference

<div class="persona-workflow">
  <div class="persona-step">
    <div>
      <strong>At the conference: captured immediately</strong>
      <p>You speak with a potential partner. Instead of pocketing a business card, you open CallPlan and dictate 15 seconds of context. Name, role, why interesting, next step. Next conversation.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="persona-step">
    <div>
      <strong>Journey home: follow-ups done</strong>
      <p>CallPlan has all contacts from the past 2 days on your list. You call 6 of them on the way home. Short, personal, concrete: "Great meeting you – when would work for a proper call?" Done.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="persona-step">
    <div>
      <strong>3 days later: remaining calls complete</strong>
      <p>Those you didn't reach are back on your list. 4 more calls. All done. Of 47 contacts you've now followed up with 40 – instead of 2.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="persona-step">
    <div>
      <strong>3 months later: real relationships</strong>
      <p>Of your 40 follow-ups, 8 became real conversations. 3 became real opportunities. One person recommended you to someone else. That's not luck – that's the 48-hour rule, automated.</p>
    </div>
